Description

This is a one term 0.5 FCE seminar course. This course is intended for graduate students in Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare (AIH) especially with the technical (CS, engineering, statistics, epidemiology) background to learn the basic principles and problems in various medical fields and to build and strengthen their skills in applying AI to medical research. The goal is to establish an essential foundation of medical concepts and problems for graduate students to take the first steps in computational research and applications of AI in medicine.

The course aims to equip you with an introduction to medicine across a variety of medical specialties. During the course, you will acquire basic knowledge about various medical concepts and hands-on experience to deploy Machine Learning (ML) algorithms. You will learn the current practices and applications of ML in medicine and understand what ML can and cannot do for medicine.

Learning Objectives

  • Become familiar with a variety of medical fields and the types of problems that can be addressed with AI.
  • Develop the ability to critically reason about problems across medical domains, including awareness of knowns and unknowns.
  • Design and implement effective solutions to specific medical questions.
  • Communicate clearly with medical experts—both about technical nuances and non-technical, domain-specific issues.
  • Present work in a way that clearly articulates its value to the intended users.
  • Structure code so it is shareable, reusable, and aligned with good software-engineering practices.
  • Situate work within the context of existing research and prior work.
  • Explain the innovative aspects of a project and justify the choices made during development.
  • Review and critically evaluate other researchers’ work in AI and healthcare.

Course Policies

Attendance & Participation

Regular attendance and active participation are essential. Students are expected to complete the required readings before class and come prepared to contribute thoughtfully to discussion.

Late Work

Weekly reflection quizzes are due at the specified time. Unless otherwise noted, a penalty of 20% per day will be applied for late submissions, up to a maximum of 5 days, after which the assignment will receive no credit.

Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be your own and must adhere to the University of Toronto’s policies on academic integrity. Plagiarism and other forms of academic misconduct will be handled according to university regulations.
